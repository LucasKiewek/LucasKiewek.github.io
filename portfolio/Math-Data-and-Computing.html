Math, Data and Computing

I always find calculus class incredibly enjoyable. This past year, I learned new concepts such as vector mathematics with linear algebra and compound function antiderivatives. What I enjoyed the most was helping my peers when they did not fully grasp a concept as it also helped me check if I had a deep enough understanding of the concepts. It also inspired me to become a calculus tutor for the coming year. Becoming a better teacher also made me a better designer because I often find myself referring to techniques I learned from explaining mathematics and physics to explain things such as biomimicry to my teammates.

The Data Analytics course taught me how to use the pandas and seaborn python libraries to visualize and analyze data, make appropriate hypotheses, use proper statistical methodology, create data models, and draw conclusions from data. I found it particularly interesting to learn about how to create linear regression models and find hidden relationships within the data.

In the Creative Electronics course, I learned how to understand and make theoretical models of circuits. My understanding of the physics behind electrical circuits solidified and I was able to explain the concepts in the course to my peers with simple yet comprehensive explanations. I no longer feel hesitant about using circuitry in design projects which should allow me to produce more comprehensive prototypes.
