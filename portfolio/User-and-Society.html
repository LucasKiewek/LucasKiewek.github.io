User and Society

During Designing for Game and Play II, I learned about different methods for motivating individuals to take certain actions. My partner and I designed “Tricky Triathlon” an ankle rehabilitation game to help athletes maintain their rehab routine in a multiplayer setting. I learned how to design different reward methods and justify them with research.

In From Idea to Design (FI2D), my team and I created ORIgame, an AR based origami game focused on teaching children to communicate and collaborate better. Having had a strong sense of what our final concept would be, we were able to focus on optimizing the user experience of our product. ORIgame was the first project in which I adjusted my design due to feedback from strangers rather than friends and family. I learned about the rules and importance behind conducting user tests ethically, especially when concerning minors. Our questionnaire was rudimentary and did not really provide crucial insights, but the process of assenging and executing a user test was a valuable learning experience. Future user tests I conduct will pay more attention to gathering useful information and recording it effectively as the mental toll of setting up the user tests will no longer be as large.
