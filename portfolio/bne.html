Part of gaining this understanding comes from understanding the user for which one is designing. During User Centered Design I learned how to make affinity diagrams from user interviews and create personas to keep the end user, their behavior, and their values in mind when designing. Furthermore, I learned how to create a conceptual model of products with no physical analogs. In previous design projects, I was designing a product for which I roughly fit the description of the target end user.

The Health and Well-being Honors track I am undertaking in the following years will have end users who are very different from me. My goal is to effectively use user interviews, affinity diagrams, and personas to have an in depth understanding of users for myself and my team. I also foresee many instances in which software will be a part of a Health and Well-being design in which case a conceptual model would be used to better communicate my ideas with the users.

Unfortunately, business and entrepreneurship did not play as large of a role this year. However, they will be a substantial part of my work as an industrial designer in the following years as I take up internships and design for real stakeholders in the honors program.
